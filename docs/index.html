Research Overview

This project develops a pipeline for generating emotionally expressive, culturally-adapted speech and 3D facial animation to serve as stimuli in a cross-cultural perception experiment. The central research question is how people weight prosody (speech melody, rhythm) versus facial expressions when interpreting emotion across Japanese, Mandarin, and English.

Stimuli are created by (1) synthesising emotional speech via state-of-the-art TTS models conditioned on a Japanese female reference speaker, (2) driving a 3D avatar with the resulting audio to produce lip-synced facial animations, and (3) inserting emotion expression either congruent or incongruent with the speech — creating the experimental contrast.

Cross-Lingual TTS — Model Comparison

Five TTS systems were evaluated for their ability to transfer the reference speaker's voice identity to a new language while preserving emotional expressiveness. Each model receives the same reference audio and target-language text; emotion is controlled via the model's native mechanism (emotion vector, reference WAV, or CFG guidance).

3D Avatar — Lip-Sync & Facial Expression Sample

Happy — Sample Stimulus

The 3D avatar is driven by the synthesised speech audio. Lip synchronisation is computed from the audio signal, while facial expression can be set congruent (happy expression + happy speech) or incongruent (neutral expression + happy speech) for the experimental condition.

  • Avatar: Unreal Engine 5 MetaHuman
  • Lip-sync: Pixel Streaming + ARKit blendshapes
  • Expression: driven from Speech-to-Face prediction model
  • Condition type: Congruent (this sample)
  • Language: English (cross-lingual from JP reference)
